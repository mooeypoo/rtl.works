Putting direction attributes in the <html> or <body> is always a good idea. The default direction of an html and body block are 'ltr', but your html might be used in other environment that have different definitions. For example:
  • Email: If you are sending an HTML email - or if anyone else is sending a piece of your website in an email - the content directionality can be decided by the client. If the email client is set to right-to-left, your content (even if it is only left-to-right) might get jumbled. Setting directionality is a good idea especially if you plan to send the HTML in an email.
  • If your site includes user-submitted content: In that case, you should consider that some of the content might be in right-to-left. In that case, you should be careful about the directionality you define in general. You might want to take a look at bidirectionality isolation. Watching out from mixed content is a good idea in any case.
